Advanced Placement (AP)

AP classes are college-level classes that are offered to students if they'd like a bigger challenge. They have the possibility to give college credit and an advantage in the application processes. Here at BSHS, we offer the following AP classes and you may click on them for more information:

AP Biology
Explores biological concepts and processes like evolution, genetics, ecology, cellular processes, etc.

AP Brawl Stars
Focuses on several winning strategies in Brawl Stars for each mode, and covers the changing meta, brawl stars history, and in-game situations.

AP Calculus AB
Calculus course that focuses on differential and intergral calculus, and covers topics like limits, intergrals, derivatives, and their applications.

AP Calculus BC
A continuation of AP Calculus AB that covers topics like series, polar function, parametric equations, etc.

AP Chemistry
Explores advanced chemical concepts and lab skills, covers topics like atmoic structure, reactions, thermodynamics, etc.

AP Computer Science A
Teaches students to understand coding through analyzing, writing, testing code while exploring concepts like modularity, control structures and variables.

AP English Language and Composition
Focuses on argumentative writing and rhetorical analysis, and students analyze and create forms of text.

AP English Literature and Composition
Involves critical reading and analysis of several literary works, and students learn to interpret literature and develop writing skills through essays.

AP Environmental Science
Explores the relationships in the natural world, and covers topic like ecosystems, biodiversity, etc.

AP Human Geography
Studies the patterns and processes that shaped human understanding of the Earth, and covers topics like population, cultural patterns, land use, etc.

AP Macroeconomics
Focuses on the principles of economics that apply to an economic system, and covers topics such as national income, economic performance measures, etc.

AP Precalculus
Prepares students for calculus, covers advanced algebraic concepts, trig, etc.

AP Physics 1 Algebra based
Covers Newton's laws, work, energy, momentum, etc.

AP Physics 2 Algebra Based
A continuation of AP Physics 1, covers fluids, thermodynamics, electricity, etc.

AP Seminar
Engages students in cross-curricular conversations, and develops critical thinking, research, collaboration and presentation skills.

AP Spanish Language and Culture
Emphasizes communicating in Spanish through studying language, culture and events.

AP Spanish Literature and Culture
Introduces stduents to representative texts from Latin American, Hispanic, etc. culture, and students develop critical reading and writing skills.

AP Statistics
Introduces students to major concepts and tools for collecting, analyzing and drawing conclusion from data.

AP Studio Art: 2-D Design
Focuses on the principles of 2D design through many techniques and media.

AP Studio Art: Drawing
Focuses on drawing, including line quality, composition and mark-making.

AP United States Government and Politics
Provides an analytical view on government and politics in the US, and covers the Constitution, political belief, parties, etc.

AP United States History
Covers American history from pre-Columbian times, to the current time, and develops understanding of global historical themes, and the ability to analyze different sources.

AP World History
Investigates important events, people, developments from 1200 CE to the present.
